Frequently Asked Questions

How do I challenge someone?
Open HeartChallenge on your Apple Watch, set a target heart rate (BPM), a duration, and an optional wager, then enter your friend's username. They'll get a notification on their wrist.
How long do they have to complete it?
Once a challenge is accepted, your friend has 12 hours to start it, then a 15-minute window to hit the target heart rate and hold it for the set duration.
How is the heart rate measured?
Live, by the Apple Watch optical heart-rate sensor through Apple HealthKit. There's no manual entry — you have to actually earn it.
How do wagers and settling up work?
The iPhone companion app keeps a ledger of who owes whom and for which challenge. You can add your Cash App $Cashtag so friends can pay you. HeartChallenge does not process or hold any money — settlement happens directly between you and your friend in Cash App.
My friend didn't receive a challenge.
Make sure both of you are signed in to iCloud and have set up a username in the app. Challenges sync through iCloud, so a working internet connection is required on both devices.
Do I need an Apple Watch?
Yes — challenges are run on the Apple Watch using its heart-rate sensor. The iPhone app is a companion for viewing your payments ledger and managing your profile.

Privacy

HeartChallenge stores your username, age (used only to calculate your max heart rate), and challenge records in your iCloud account via Apple CloudKit. Heart-rate data is read on-device through HealthKit during a challenge and is not stored on our servers. We do not sell your data. Your Cash App $Cashtag, if you add one, is shared only with friends you have a challenge with so they can pay you.
