HeartChallenge — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 6, 2026

HeartChallenge ("we," "us," or "the app") respects your privacy. This policy explains what information the app handles and how it is used.

Information the app handles

Profile. A username you choose and your age. Your age is used only to calculate your maximum heart rate for challenges.

Challenge data. Challenges you send and receive, including the heart-rate target, duration, status, and timestamps.

Heart-rate data. During a challenge, the app reads your heart rate live from the Apple Watch sensor through Apple HealthKit.

How your data is stored

Your profile and challenges are stored in Apple iCloud using CloudKit, tied to your Apple ID. Because the game is played between people, this information is kept in a shared public CloudKit database so the friends you challenge can receive challenges and see results.

We do not run our own servers that collect your personal data — the app relies on Apple's iCloud/CloudKit infrastructure.

HealthKit

HeartChallenge uses Apple HealthKit to read heart-rate data solely to determine challenge outcomes in real time. Heart-rate readings are processed on your device and are not transmitted to or stored on our servers or in iCloud. We never use HealthKit data for advertising and never sell or share it with third parties.

Points and purchases

HeartChallenge uses in-app points, not money, to track challenges. Points are stored on your device. You may optionally buy point packs through Apple's In-App Purchase; those transactions are processed by Apple, and we do not receive or store your payment details.

Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. Your username, age-derived maximum heart rate, and challenge details are visible — through CloudKit — to the specific friends you challenge or who challenge you, so the game can function.

Data retention and deletion

Your data persists in your iCloud account. You can clear your local challenge history by deleting the app, and you can request deletion of your public profile by emailing us. We do not retain your heart-rate data.

Children

HeartChallenge is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected here with a new effective date.
